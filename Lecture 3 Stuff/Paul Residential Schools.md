# Disruption of family structures

- The first nations families had distinct structures with designated roles for all people
- Removal of children destroyed these family structures, with no children to nurture and no one to take care of the children
- This resulted in Elsie's mother losing any instinct for being a mother bringing what she experienced as a child in the residential schools into her being a mother to her kids

## Institutionalization and Dehumanization
- Described as jails or labour camps
- Designed to strip children of their individuality and mold them into obedient subjects
- Children were assigned numbers instead of names making them feel like prisoners instead of people
- Interaction between siblings was forbidden even looking at her cousins was forbidden and would result in being physically punished
- Described as being tiny little soldiers

## Cultural Erasure and Language loss
- Primary purpose to eradicate the indigenous culture and language. Children speaking native tongue were strictly punished
- Traditional stuff was banned such as cedar bough cleansing
- By erasing the language people are almost entirely unable to connect to the culture because so much of the culture is language

## Trauma, Abuse, and Neglect
- physical and emotional abuse, food neglect. Environment of fear
- Super severe punishment for even the smallest things
- many tried to escape but were caught 

## Intergenerational Trauma (The Cycle of Abuse)
- Trauma suffered created a cylce of abuse where they dont know warmth and only know the strict environment that they were a part of.
- Elsie's husband kept a strap hanging on the wall to threaten the kids. He learned it from the school but never used it.
- Many survivors became hateful parents that never showed love to their own kids.

## Critique of Financial Compensation vs healing
- Elsie is critical of the financial payments of reconciliation (the common experience payment), Money cannot fix the deep psychological scars and can often exacerbate the problems like alcoholism
- Giving money to people with massive problems is like giving them poison, many drank themselves to death
- Elsie believes money should have gone to councillors to help deal with the problems

## Resilience and "Two Worlds" Education
- Elsie emphasizes resilience 
- Future generations should embrace their culture as well as the tools of the modern day
- To regain their pride in their culture
